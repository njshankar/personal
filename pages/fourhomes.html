Four Homes

Yesterday, on the shuttle bus from the office, I was able to have a nice chat with an epidemiologist colleague of mine. He told me about how he came from Hyderabad, that big city in southern India. When he was seven years old, his family moved all the way to England. His parents were doctors, and back then, if you were coming from abroad, you could only work at a clinic for six months at a time. So the family moved around many times over the course of the next six to seven years. Growing up in that way, it was difficult to feel like any place was home.

Today, I also visited Claremont. Whenever I head that way, the nostalgic feeling always comes rushing back. By my count, I have had four homes.

Home #1: Chicago

This one was the first and longest. In contrast to my kindly colleage, I was blessed with a stable home for my first eighteen years of life. Growing up in a quiet little corner of town, I never lacked for anything that wasn't inside myself. Strangely though, I never appreciated this home until I had left for college. Especially during COVID, I was thankful to have a refuge to return to. My sophomore and junior years were my most productive years of study.
